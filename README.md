# Integrated Research Environment for Recombinant Antigen-Based Diagnostic Development

> A researcher-centered platform that unifies the fragmented workflow of recombinant antigen discovery, development, validation, and deployment into a single evidence-driven scientific environment.

## Overview

The Integrated Research Environment (IRE) is an open scientific software platform designed to support the systematic development of recombinant antigen-based diagnostics for infectious diseases.

Researchers currently perform this work across numerous independent tools, including biological databases, literature repositories, sequence analysis software, structural prediction platforms, spreadsheets, laboratory records, and artificial intelligence assistants. This fragmented workflow reduces reproducibility, complicates decision-making, and increases the time required to progress from pathogen knowledge to a validated diagnostic assay.

IRE addresses this challenge by providing a unified environment where researchers can organize knowledge, perform computational analyses, collect evidence, compare candidate antigens, document scientific reasoning, and make evidence-based decisions throughout the diagnostic development lifecycle.

Although the initial implementation focuses on African Animal Trypanosomiasis (AAT), the platform is designed to remain extensible to other infectious diseases.

---

## Vision

To become an integrated scientific research environment that supports the complete lifecycle of recombinant antigen-based diagnostic development while promoting reproducibility, transparency, collaboration, and evidence-based scientific decision-making.

---

## Core Research Question

> **How can computational biology, artificial intelligence, and reproducible scientific workflows be integrated into a unified framework for the systematic discovery, development, validation, and deployment of recombinant antigen-based diagnostics for infectious diseases?**

## Scientific Workflow

```text
Pathogen Knowledge
        ↓
Candidate Antigen Discovery
        ↓
Protein Selection
        ↓
Sequence Engineering
        ↓
Gene Synthesis
        ↓
Plasmid Construction
        ↓
Protein Expression
        ↓
Protein Purification
        ↓
Immunization
        ↓
Diagnostic Development
        ↓
Diagnostic Validation
        ↓
Field Deployment
```

Each stage will be implemented as a dedicated **Research Studio** within the platform.

---

## Architectural Principles

The platform is built around several core principles:

* Researcher-centered design
* Evidence-centric architecture
* Scientific reproducibility
* Explainable artificial intelligence
* Modular scientific capabilities
* Plugin-based extensibility
* Human-guided scientific decision-making
* Long-term maintainability

## Current Status

🚧 **Early Development**

The current focus is establishing the platform architecture and implementing the foundational infrastructure before introducing biological functionality.
